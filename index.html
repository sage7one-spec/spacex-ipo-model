How it works. Each of 10,000 paths samples a Day-1 closing market cap from the live Polymarket distribution → a closing price (cap ÷ shares outstanding), then re-centers it toward the blend of the Hyperliquid xyz:SPCX mark and the Polymarket-implied price (weight slider). The opening print is drawn from historical mega-IPO open-to-close behavior, then a Brownian-bridge random walk (scaled by your intraday-volatility setting) connects the open to that close across 8 hourly steps. The fan shows the percentile envelope of those paths; the low/high charts show each path's intraday extremes.

Honest read. At $135 with a blended close near the live mark, the close is usually green — but the intraday ride can still dip below your entry early before settling higher, and a thin minority of paths close red. The model says "probably up, possibly bumpy," not "guaranteed." It cannot predict a specific halt, circuit-breaker, or news shock.

About the Execution Plan. It models fills at 8 intraday steps (an approximation of true intraday highs/lows) and assumes limit orders fill at the rung price, the core sells at the opening print, and stops fill at the stop price — real fills can gap through a stop in a fast move or halt, so the "hold more" scenarios carry more downside than the model shows. The ~17% chance of a sub-$135 sale is largely irreducible: it is roughly the model's probability that SPCX is below your $135 basis at the open or the close, which a must-be-flat-by-close plan cannot avoid. Per this model the highest-expected-value Day-1 move is simply to sell into the open; the laddered scenarios trade a small expected haircut for upside participation plus a defined floor. The 20-day hold fan (Case C) defaults to zero drift (a martingale); the optional drift-weight and Polymarket-anchor sliders are planning aids, not forecasts. Order types, the on-close (MOC/LOC) cutoff, and stop behavior on a first-day IPO must be confirmed in your own Fidelity account; selling within 15 calendar days is a flip (6-month IPO-access block, first offense).
